In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

For each of the following projections, I used the natural earth background provided on sakai. I clicked on the EPSG button in the bottom bar of QGIS, followed by the CRS tab in the project properties window, to select a projection. I used the indicatrix tile to engage Tissot's Indicatrix.

WGS84 Projection

The WGS84 (Mercator) projection is used in classrooms and for navigation because it preserves area at low lattitudes and direction at all latitiudes. The Mercator projection is misleading because it exaggerates area near the poles.

Aitoff Projection

The Aitoff projection is like the Mercator projection in that it exaggerates area near the poles. The Aitoff projection is unline the Mercator projection because it does not have straight latitude/longitude lines. As a result, it does not preserve direction with precision comparable to the Mercator projection.

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

The Pseudo-Mercator projection preserves area at low lattitudes and preserves direction at all latitiudes, making it useful for navigation. However, this projection distorts area at high latitudes. For example, Greenland appears to be approximately the size of Africa even though Greenland is several magnitudes smaller than Africa.

Sphere Winkel Projection

The Sphere Winkel projection distorts area somewhat at the poles. However, the distortion gradient is not as severe as what is seen in the Mercator projection. The Sphere Winkel projection preserves latitudinal direction because it has straight latitude lines, and it does not preserve longitudinal direction because the longitude lines are curved.

World Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

Like the Mercator projection, the World Cylindrical Equal Area projection preserves latitudinal and longitudinal direction. Also like the Mercator projection, the World Cylindrical Equal Area projection distorts land area at the poles. The World Cylindrical Equal Area projection is more latitudinally stretched at the poles than the Mercator projection.

World Equidistant Conic Projection

The World Equidistant Conic projection minimally distorts area at the north pole, but the latitudinal distortion increases with proximity to the south pole.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

Similarly to the World Equidistant Conic projection, the North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant projection minimally distorts area at the north pole, and the distortion increases with proximity to the south pole.

World Mollweide Projection

The World Mollweide projection has a relatively uniform degree of distortion at all latitudes/longitudes, and does not preserve direction because the latitude and longitude lines are curved.

World Eckert VI Projection

But for the flat cutoff at either pole, the World Eckert VI projection appears to be indentical to the World Mollweide projection (i.e. uniform distortion at all latitudes/longitudes, direction not preserved).
